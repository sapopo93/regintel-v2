## 4. COMPETITIVE BATTLE CARDS

### vs. Log My Care / Nourish Care / Person Centred Software (Care Management Systems)

**Their pitch:** "Manage your care digitally — care records, medication, rostering, compliance."

**Their gap:** They are care management systems. They store and manage care delivery. They do NOT audit evidence against SAF quality statements, run mock CQC inspections, generate root cause analysis, or alert you when a peer provider gets an RI rating. Their "compliance" features are document libraries and task trackers.

**Your move:** "Log my Care tracks that you did the care. RegIntel proves that your care meets CQC's quality standards. Those are different products. You probably need both — RegIntel pulls evidence from your care management system and audits it against SAF."

**Don't fight them. Position as complementary.** Partner opportunity: RegIntel as the compliance intelligence layer on top of care management systems.

---

### vs. QCS (Quality Compliance Systems — Policy Library)

**Their pitch:** "3,500+ CQC-compliant policy templates, regularly updated."

**Their gap:** QCS gives you compliant policies. It doesn't tell you whether your staff follow them, whether your evidence proves it, or what a CQC inspector would actually find in your facility. Policies are necessary but not sufficient — CQC inspects lived experience, not documents.

**Your move:** "QCS tells you what your policies should say. RegIntel tells you whether your evidence actually proves those policies exist in practice. Providers get RI ratings with perfect QCS policies because their evidence doesn't match their paperwork."

**Killer example:** A provider's infection control policy (from QCS) says procedures were last updated in 2023. RegIntel's document audit catches that it references superseded 2019 PHE guidance. QCS didn't catch it. The inspector would.

---

### vs. Compliance Consultants

**Their pitch:** "Specialist expertise, personal service, bespoke reports."

**Their gap:** Episodic (quarterly visits), expensive (£400–1,500/day), judgment-based (not reproducible), can't monitor 15 peer providers simultaneously, no cryptographic audit trail.

**Your move:** "RegIntel doesn't replace your consultant — it makes them 3x more effective. They spend their day on strategy, not reading policies. And for providers who can't afford a consultant, RegIntel gives them consultant-level analysis at 1/10th the cost."

**Pricing anchor:** RegIntel Starter at £299/month = less than 1 hour of consultant time per month. Blue Ocean report at £0 additional = replaces a full-day consultant engagement.

---

### vs. Generic GRC (Vanta, Drata, OneTrust)

**Don't fight this battle.** These tools are for SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR. Care providers don't buy Vanta. If you encounter them, say: "Those tools are built for IT compliance frameworks. CQC uses the Single Assessment Framework — 34 quality statements, professional judgment, lived experience evaluation. It's a fundamentally different regulatory environment. RegIntel is purpose-built for it."
